Hangzhou is the capital and largest city of Zhejiang Province in Eastern China. Known for its natural beauty and historical and cultural heritages, it is one of the most famous tourist cities in China.

Famed for its natural scenery, especiall those around West Lake area, Hangzhou has been immortalized by countless poets and artists. The city was also the capital of the Southern Song Dynasty from 1127 until the Mongol invasion of 1276. Even Marco Polo claimed to have passed through, calling it beyond dispute the finest and the noblest in the world. Since June 2011, the West Lake Cultural Landscape has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site. With the gradual silting up of its harbor, much of the city's trade and industry passed north to nearby Shanghai, but the city still has a bustling population of 8.8 million and ranks as one of China's most popular tourist attractions.
